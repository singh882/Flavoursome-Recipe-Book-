Dosa

SERVINGS 3/4    PREP TIME 15 MINS     COOK TIME 10 MINS    

Crispy and delicious dosa Recipe, quick & easy to knock together in minutes and has everyone salivating every time. Give it a go and see for yourself how easy it is!

Ingredients

  • For the Dosa Batter:
    • 1 cup rice

    • 1/4 cup urad dal (split black gram)

    • 1/4 teaspoon fenugreek seeds (optional)

    • Salt to taste

    • Water (for soaking and grinding)

  • For the Masala Filling:
    • 1 cup paneer/aloo (crumbled or grated)

    • 1 medium onion (finely chopped)

    • 1-2 green chilies (finely chopped)

    • 1/2 teaspoon ginger-garlic paste

    • 1/2 teaspoon cumin seeds

    • 1/2 teaspoon garam masala

    • Salt to taste

    • 2 tablespoons oil or ghee (for cooking)

    • Fresh cilantro (chopped, for garnish)

Instructions

  1. Rinse the rice and urad dal separately under cold water until the water runs clear.
  2. Soak the rice, urad dal, and fenugreek seeds (if using) in water for at least 4-6 hours or overnight.
  3. Drain the soaked ingredients and blend them with water to form a smooth batter. The consistency should be similar to pancake batter.
  4. Transfer the batter to a bowl, cover, and let it ferment in a warm place for 8-12 hours or until it doubles in size.
  5. Once fermented, add salt to taste and mix gently.
  6. For the masala filling, heat oil in a pan and add cumin seeds. Once they splutter, add chopped onions and green chilies.
  7. Saut√© until the onions turn translucent, then add ginger-garlic paste and cook for another minute.
  8. Add crumbled paneer or aloo, garam masala, and salt. Mix well and cook for 2-3 minutes. Remove from heat and garnish with cilantro.
  9. Heat a non-stick or cast-iron skillet over medium heat. Pour a ladleful of dosa batter and spread it in a circular motion.
  10. Drizzle some oil or ghee around the edges and cook until the dosa is golden brown.
  11. Add a spoonful of the paneer or aloo filling to one half of the dosa and fold it over. Cook for another minute, then serve hot.
